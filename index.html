Elgin Stray Cat Map

A spatial mapping application built for the Elgin community — turning scattered reports of missing and stray cats into one shared, living map.

Sightings used to live in social media threads and word of mouth, so rescue efforts overlapped and leads went cold. Automated Address Assignment for Water Service Lines

A geoprocessing tool that assigns street addresses and location IDs to water service lines by reading the direction the pipe points — replacing a manual, one-record-at-a-time data entry task.

Elgin's water utility GIS has tens of thousands of service lines, each needing the address and LOCID of the house it serves. That attribution was entirely manual — click a line, find the house, type two fields, move on. The obvious automation, nearest-neighbor matching, fails on deep-setback lots where the margin between the correct house and the neighbor shrinks to as little as 12.5 feet.

The insight

Service lines run perpendicular from the main, through the buffalo box, aimed at the house they serve. The geometry already encodes the answer — nearest-neighbor throws it away by measuring magnitude and discarding direction. Instead of asking which house is closest, the tool asks which house the line points at.

How it works

  • Extracts both endpoints and identifies the house end versus the street end
  • Computes the bearing from street to house and casts a ray along it
  • The footprint the ray intersects is the match — setback depth stops mattering
  • Falls back to distance with an ambiguity margin, flagging rather than guessing

Designed to be distrusted

  • Preview mode on by default — full analysis, zero edits
  • Ambiguous records flagged for human review, never guessed at
  • Every decision logged to CSV with method, distances, and margin
  • Flagged records auto-selected in the map as the operator's to-do list
